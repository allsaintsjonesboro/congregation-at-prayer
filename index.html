Catechism — The First Petition
⌄

The Our Father: First Petition

Hallowed be Thy name. Preschool +

What does this mean? 2nd Grade +

God's name is certainly holy in itself, but we pray in this petition that it may be kept holy among us also.

How is God's name kept holy? 3rd Grade +

God's name is kept holy when the Word of God is taught in its truth and purity, and we, as the children of God, also lead holy lives according to it. Help us to do this, dear Father in heaven! But anyone who teaches or lives contrary to God's Word profanes the name of God among us. Protect us from this, heavenly Father!

Catechesis Notes

When you were baptized — whether that was a long, long time ago, or relatively recently — the triune name of God, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, was joined to water, and through it, you were made a child of God.

Whenever God's name is attached to something, that thing is empowered with all the holiness of God: Baptism, Absolution, the Gospel, Holy Communion — the Divine Liturgy as a whole. God's name is holy because it's His name; we can't make it any more or less so.

However, we pray in this petition that God would strengthen us to keep His name holy among us also. That means using rightly the things to which God has attached His name. So when God's Word is taught in its truth and purity, when we hear it gladly and give thanks for His gifts, when we turn away from temptation and live according to God's good and gracious will — these are the ways that we keep God's name holy among us. Likewise, when we daily remember our baptism, confess our sins and receive the holy absolution, come regularly to hear the Gospel proclaimed, and receive the Eucharist often, God's name is kept holy among us. We pray in this petition that God in His mercy and grace would help us to do these things.

On the other hand, when we despise those things, God's name is profaned. When the Word of God is taught falsely and when we willingly listen to false doctrine, when we neglect the remembrance of our Baptism and the regular reception of the Means of Grace, when we use the freedom that we have in Christ to lead unholy lives — all of these things are a rejection of the holiness of God and the name He has placed on us in our Baptism. Indeed, they are a rejection of God Himself. We pray in this petition that God would protect us from this and preserve us in the one true faith.

Prayer for Ourselves and Others
⌄
Sunday
Pray for the joy of the resurrection among us; for the fruit of faith nourished by the Word and Sacraments.
Monday
Pray for faith to live in the promises of Holy Baptism; for one's calling and daily work; for the unemployed; for the salvation and well-being of our neighbors; for schools, colleges, and seminaries; for good government and for peace.
Tuesday
Pray for deliverance against temptation and evil; for the addicted and despairing, the tortured and oppressed; for those struggling with besetting sins.
Wednesday
Pray for marriage and family, that husbands and wives, parents and children live in ordered harmony according to the Word of God; for parents who must raise children alone; for our communities and neighborhoods.
Thursday
Pray for the Church and her pastors; for teachers, deacons, deaconesses, and other church workers; for missionaries and for all who serve the Church; for fruitful and salutary use of the blessed sacrament of Christ's body and blood.
Friday
Pray for the preaching of the holy cross of our Lord Jesus Christ and for the spread of His knowledge throughout the whole world; for the persecuted and oppressed; for the sick and dying.
Saturday
Pray for faithfulness to the end; for the renewal of those who are withering in the faith or have fallen away; for receptive hearts and minds to God's Word on the Lord's Day; for pastors and people as they prepare to administer and receive Christ's holy gifts.

Hymn of the Week
⌄
"God's Own Child, I Gladly Say It" — LSB 594 (sts. 1, 2, 5)
Stanza 1
God's own child, I gladly say it:
I am baptized into Christ!
He, because I could not pay it,
Gave my full redemption price.
Do I need earth's treasures many?
I have one worth more than any
That brought me salvation free
Lasting to eternity!
Stanza 2
Sin, disturb my soul no longer:
I am baptized into Christ!
I have comfort even stronger:
Jesus' cleansing sacrifice.
Should a guilty conscience seize me
Since my Baptism did release me
In a dear forgiving flood,
Sprinkling me with Jesus' blood?
Stanza 5
There is nothing worth comparing
To this lifelong comfort sure!
Open-eyed my grave is staring:
Even there I'll sleep secure.
Though my flesh awaits its raising,
Still my soul continues praising:
I am baptized into Christ;
I'm a child of paradise!

Erdmann Neumeister, 1671–1756
